Mental wellbeing is often confused with having to feel happy all the time, however, it is more about feeling good about yourself and being able to cope with the challenges we face with a sense of purpose. Although it is normal to feel stressed or negative, these feelings can develop into a serious mental health problem when neglected. A recent report from the American Psychological Association shows a sharp increase in severe psychological problems being reported among students (Lee). Stress eats away at us, eroding our sense of wellbeing and self. We must remember that there isn't a test score, grade, college, or job title that is more important than a student's mental, physical, and emotional well-being. Grades and accomplishments have their place, but your wellbeing will always be more important than anything else.

Meditation and mindful breathing are simple, inexpensive, ways to enhance our mental wellbeing and can be practiced wherever you are. Spending even a few minutes in meditation can help restore your calm, inner peace, and improve your overall well being. Both practices create a soothing experience meant to help eliminate the jumbled thoughts in our mind that may cause us any negative feelings of stress and discomfort. One of the beautiful things about meditation and mindful breathing is that the various benefits don’t end once you stop; they help you go through your day more calmly and clearly so that you feel more at peace. Remember that there’s no right or wrong way to meditate or one way to take mindful breaths. What matters is that you do what helps you reduce your stress and have a stronger sense of well being.
